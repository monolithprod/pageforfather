The Epic of Martin

Born in a bygone age, in a place long forgotten.
A prophecy spoke of a Pitcairn and Watson,
Two highlanders who would fight through the cold,
and unite together to break the mold,
of a family mired in divorce and defeat,
to always stay true, through rain snow and sleet.
This monolith of a man, took his lady by hand,
and together they formulated a plan,
to raise a family down south in England.

Many years passed and babies were born,
and yet still they lived in the land of forlorn,
What tragedy blooms when surrounded by neds,
This powerful man grew tired of dickheads,
With final child born, he didn't have long,
before his oldest could soon start to do wrong,
so they gathered their things and set off to move on.

For Martin to take his family there,
he had to seek out Hercules the bear,
500 pounds of flesh muscle and hair,
the beast roared, "submit me if you dare!"
But Martin was quicker than bats out of hell,
and wrestled the monster until it fell,
beast begging for mercy, Martin doesn't sell,
he just laughed, standing tall alongside the death knell.

With thunderous applause, Martin had won the right,
to seek comfort down south, and forget the fight,
to raise his family far away from the blight,
like the rough dodgy Scotsmen that came out at night.
The journey was long, but no terrible plight,
for the only horror that could give them fright,
was the boring old neighbours chatting their trite,
and the contractors cutting the fields grass like shite.

It was not before long that he could finally see,
the journey they made to the land of tea,
was the right choice to make for his family to be,
happy and prosperous, forever peacefully.

Shame about the middle child.

With love, Matthew
